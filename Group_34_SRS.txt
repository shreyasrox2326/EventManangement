1 Introduction
1.1 Purpose
This project is for the development of an Event Management & Ticketing System, which
will be used for managing events such as Conferrences, Concerts, Workshops, etc. As
of now, all the events described in the above problem statement are being managed
through counter registrations or manual ticketing systems. However, this is causing
many problems, including duplicate registrations, untidy registrations etc.
So, in short, the aim of EMTS is to move all this into a single online solution, where the
events can be managed by the people organizing them. So, for the customers, basically,
they have to be able to access the event, they have to be able to buy the tickets online,
they have to have a QR or a Barcode ticket to get into the events, etc., without any
kind of confusion.
1.2 Document Conventions
To keep this project description intelligible, a number of terms are used throughout:
(a)Organizer:the creator/team of the event
(b)EMTS:Event Management and Ticketing System
(c)Customer:Any person browsing the event and booking tickets.
(d)Staff:Venue staff, validates by scanning the ticket
(e)Types of tickets:VIP, Regular, Student concession, Complimentary
(f)Refund Rules:The rules for refunds are specified using everyday time-based
vocabulary (for example, ”full refund before 7 days”)
1.3 Intended Audience
The intended audience is event organisers, corporates, customers who want to buy tickets
to events and event staff.
5

--- PAGE 6 ---
1.4 Additional Information
EMTS covers the whole life cycle of the event. Organizers can add events that contain
information such as type, venue, capacity, date, duration, and price of tickets in advance.
There can be different categories of tickets, such as paid and/or free for VIPs, sponsors,
and staff. There can be bulk bookings, which are available at a discount.
The customers can look at the events coming up, check the availlability of these, and
can also make an online booking and obtain a ticket with a unique QR/Barcode. In the
case of cancellations, the refunds will be processed according to the rules, where the full
refund has to be made before the completion of 7 days, and 50% before the completion
of 48 hours, and no refunds will be made after this period.
During the process, it is also possible for the tickets to be scanned in order to verify
attendance. There is also the option of viewing the revenue by type of ticket, occupancy,
and non-shows report for offline agents after the event, as well as the performance report
for the sales agents. Additionally, the application includes a tracker where expenses can
be entered against fees, venues, equipment, etc., in the form of budgets.

2 Overall Description
2.1 Product Perspective
The Event Management and Ticketing System (EMTS) is a web-based application, and
mobile based application, which has the aim of coordinating the whole event planning
process, ticketing and coordinating the attendees to conferences, concerts and workshops.
This does away with manual ticketing of events as it is done through a counter in addition
to physical tickets. EMTS is a combined platform that is employed to initiate interplay
between various stakeholders, including organizers, attendees, venue staff, corporate
clients, and administrators. EMTS is applied as a unified system of organizing the
realization of events, the sale of tickets, and payment, validation, and analysis.
The system communicates with other peripheral elements like:
(a) Online payment gateways (e.g., Razorpay/Stripe/ PayU),
(b) Email notification service, SMS notification service,
(c) The QR/Barcode scanners in places,
(d) Databases and cache layers based on the cloud.
Its architecture revolves around a micro services (modular and service oriented) model
which allows it to scale, be highly available, and maintainable. It is primarily based on
the Java(Spring boot) and MySQL/PostgreSQL, Redis, and the message queuing system
like RabbitMQ/Kafka.
2.2 Product Functions
The key services proposed by EMTS are:
2.2.1 Event Creation & Management
(a) Organizers are able to generate, update, publish and maintain events.
(b) Multitask-support (Conference, Concert, Workshop).
(c) Capacity planning and venue management.

cial management.
(b)Customer / Attendee:Browse events, purchase tickets, pay and attend events.

reliably.

(d)RabbitMQ/Kafka in the case of asynchronous notifications:To dispose of
booking confirmations and alerts fast without slackening the system.

(a) The system has to be created in Java based on Spring Boot to pursue the enterprise
development practices in line with the industry standards.

(c) Bcrypt hashing should be used to store passwords to secure the user credentials in

(d) QR codes should have coded payloads in order to avoid manipulation, so bogus
tickets can not be created and used again.

3 External Interface Requirements
This section details how the Event Management and Ticketing System (EMTS) interacts
with human users, specialized hardware, other software systems, and the underlying
communication network.
3.1 User Interfaces
The system provides a responsive web-based interface built for three distinct user roles:
Customers, Organizers/Staff, and System Administrators.
(a)Customer Web Portal:The layout focuses on a ”Search-and-Select” flow. A
main dashboard displays featured events like Concerts, Conferences, Workshops.
The booking interface features a dynamic seating map where users can click on
available sections to choose their ticket category (VIP, Regular, etc.). Upon pay-
ment, a confirmation screen displays the unique QR code for immediate download
or viewing.
(b)Organizer Management Dashboard:Designed for desktop use, this UI follows
a data-heavy sidebar navigation. Key screens include ”Event Creation” (form-
based with validation for capacity and dates) and a ”Financial Tracker” screen
that uses bar charts to compare Budget vs. Actual expenses.
(c)Staff Entry-Scan View:A mobile-optimized view for venue staff. It features a
simplified interface dominated by a ”Camera Scan” window for ticket validation
and a live numerical counter showing real-time attendance vs. total capacity.
3.2 Hardware Interfaces
The EMTS interacts with external hardware primarily for ticketing validation and print-
ing:
(a)QR/Barcode Scanners:The system supports dedicated handheld HID (Human
Interface Device) scanners used by staff at venue entrances. These devices connect
via USB or Bluetooth and act as keyboard input emulators for the Java application
to process the ticket’s unique ID.
12

--- PAGE 13 ---
3.3 Software Interfaces
The application depends on the following software integrations to maintain data integrity
and handle financial transactions:
(a)Database Management System:The system will interface with a MySQL or
PostgreSQL database using JDBC (Java Database Connectivity). This handles all
persistent storage for event details, bulk booking records, and attendance logs.
(b)Payment Gateway API:The system will communicate with a third-party pay-
ment processor (such as Razorpay) via their Java SDK. This handles secure credit
card processing and automatic refund execution based on the logic defined in the
project scope.
(c)Mail/Notification Server:An integration with an SMTP server or an API like
SendGrid via the JavaMail API to send digital tickets and QR codes to customer
email addresses automatically after booking.
3.4 Communication Protocols and Interfaces
All communications within the system and to external services are strictly governed by
industry-standard protocols to ensure security and speed:
(a)HTTPS:All data transmission between the client-side browser and the Java back-
end will be encrypted using TLS/SSL via HTTPS (Port 443).
(b)RESTful APIs:Communication between the front-end and the Java business
logic is conducted via RESTful web services. All data is exchanged in JSON
(JavaScript Object Notation) format for efficiency.
(c)WebSockets:To fulfill the requirement for ”real-time attendance data,” the sys-
tem may use WebSockets to push live entry counts from the staff scanning app to
the organizer dashboard without requiring a page refresh.
(d)TCP/IP:Standard protocol for all internal database connections and server-to-
server API calls.

4 System Features
4.1 Create Event
4.1.1 Description
Create event allows the organizer to register a new event into the EMTS. The organizer
would need to provide all relevant information about the event, including the name,
type, venue, capacity, ticket price and date(s) and duration. Following which, the users
will be able to purchase tickets for the event.
Creation of events is a critical feature of the EMTS.
4.1.2 Action/Result
For the creation of an event, the organizer will be presented with a form, into which
they will be required to fill all the relevant fields and press submit. After this the system
validates all inputs, like capacity>0, date>current date, price≥0 etc.
If the inputs are valid, the event is added to the database with a unique event id.
The event then becomes visible in the upcoming events list. If invalid, an error message
is displayed and the erroneous fields are highlighted in red, prompting the organizer to
correct them.
4.1.3 Functional Requirements
(a) The system shall allow the user to input all details of the event in a form like
Name, Type, Venue, Capacity (int), ticket price, event date(s) (may be multi-day
or recurring), start and end timings, description.
(b) The system shall support the creation of multiple ticket categories each with dif-
ferent prices, quantity, start of sale date, for each event.
(c) The system shall allow the organizer to specify a refund policy in case of cancella-
tion, this may range from no refund, to a partial or full refund depending on the
amount of time remaining to the start of the event.
(d) The system shall validate that the capacity is a positive number, that the date is
not in the past, that the ticket prices are non-negative, and that the name/venue
fields are not empty.
(e) The system shall notify the user (organizer) by means of a popup in case validation
fails and highlight erroneous fields in red, prompting the user to make corrections.

--- PAGE 15 ---
(f) The system shall display a success message in case the event is created successfully.
(g) The system shall store the created event in the database.
4.2 Book Ticket
4.2.1 Description
Book ticket allows the user to browse upcoming events, check availability of seats and
ticket prices, and once the sales window starts, the users will be able to book tickets and
make payments for the same.
Booking tickets is a core functionality of the EMTS.
4.2.2 Action and Result
Users can scroll through a list of upcoming events, search and filter them on the basis of
date, price, name, venue. They will click the event they are interested in and be directed
to a screen showing all the details about the event, like description, name, time, venue,
cancellation policy.
If the sales window is open, they may press the buy ticket button which opens a
menu showing the available ticket types with their corresponding prices. They will enter
the quantity of tickets that they wish to buy. A final cost of the tickets they have
selected will be displayed. They will confirm their selection and then proceed to fill out
a form with their email address, name and phone number. After confirming their wish to
purchase the tickets, a payment gateway will open. Once the payment has been made,
the database will be updated with the ticket information, and a confirmation email will
be sent to the user with the ticket.
4.2.3 Functional Requirements
(a) The system shall display on a screen all upcoming events.
(b) The system shall allow the user to search, filter and sort through the events.
(c) The system shall display detailed event information when the user clicks on an
event.
(d) The system shall display a buy ticket option if the sales window of the event is
open.
(e) The system shall display all available ticket categories and their prices when the
user clicks the buy ticket button.
(f) The system shall allow the user to specify the number of tickets that they wish to
purchase and show total cost of the tickets.
15

--- PAGE 16 ---
(g) The system shall not allow the user to enter a quantity of tickets that is greater
than the quantity that is unsold.
(h) The system shall allow the user to confirm their wish to purchase tickets and then
redirect them to a payment gateway.
(i) The system shall confirm successful payment before generating tickets.
(j) The system shall update the database with the record of the ticket sale.
(k) The system shall send an email to the customer with a confirmation and attach
the QR code/Bar code of the ticket.
(l) The system shall not generate tickets if payment fails.
4.3 Cancel Ticket
4.3.1 Description
Cancellation of the tickets is allowed subject to the cancellation policy specified by the
organizer of the event.
This is not a core functionality to allow the EMTS to function, but is important for
user satisfaction and peace of mind.
4.3.2 Action and Result
The user may see a list of their purchased tickets in a separate section of the EMTS.
Wherein they may click on a ticket and choose to cancel it subject to the cancellation
policy specified by the organizer at the time of purchase. The system will then set
the status of the ticket in the database as cancelled, and notify the user about the
cancellation via email.
4.3.3 Functional Requirements
(a) The system shall allow the user to see a list of their purchase history in a separate
section of their GUI.
(b) The system shall allow the user to click at any item in this list and see the details
of the ticket purchase like quantity, cost, ticket category in a popup window.
(c) The system shall also display a cancel ticket button in the same window.
(d) The system shall allow the user to click this button and will prompt the user to
confirm their cancellation once again. The system shall also notify the user of the
refund policy at this moment.
(e) The system shall notify the user of the cancellation via email and their refund
status if applicable.

(f) The system shall automatically initiate the refund according to the refund policy
if applicable.
4.4 Non-Ordinary Ticket Booking (bulk, VIP, complementary)
4.4.1 Description
The system will allow the organizer of the event to reserve tickets for VIP, Sponsors and
Staff via a similar ticket booking UI as described earlier. The system will also allow the
organizer to bulk book tickets on behalf of organizations.
Bulk booking is a rarely used feature and is not a core functionality of the EMTS.
4.4.2 Action and Result
The organizer of the event will be able to see all events that they have organized in
a separate section of the UI. Upon clicking the event they will be able to see options
to manually create reserved ticket categories like staff, VIP, complementary etc. and
reserve a desired number of tickets for their own usage.
If the organizer has been contacted by an organization for bulk ticket bookings, they
may also manually book tickets in bulk on behalf of the organization at their agreed
ticket price. The reservation is confirmed once the organization makes the payment
successfully. In either case, the database of tickets gets updated accordingly and the
ticket holders are sent an email notification of their reservation with the ticket QR/bar
code.
4.4.3 Functional Requirements
(a) The system shall allow the organiser to view all their organised events in a separate
section of the UI.
(b) The system shall allow the organiser to click at any event within the list and will
then show a new window where details of the event are shown.
(c) The system will show options on the screen to enable the organizer to make bulk
bookings or create special tickets for internal usage, VIPs or complementary offers.
(d) The system shall accept input from the organizer about the quantity and type of
ticket to be allocated in the case of bulk bookings. The organizer will also be asked
to provide the email of the organization that is the recipient of these tickets.
(e) The system shall validate that the number and type of tickets selected do not
exceed the remaining capacity of the venue.
(f) The system shall send a payment gateway link to the organization via email; only
upon successful completion of the payment will they receive their tickets via email.

(h) The system shall enable the organizer to manually create a desired number of

4.5 Entry Validation and Attendance
4.5.1 Description
The entry of guests will be controlled and tickets will be verified by the scanning of the
unique QR/Bar code of each ticket.
Validation of tickets and marking attendance is a core functionality of the EMTS
without which the system cannot be effectively automated.
4.5.2 Action and Result
At the entry gates, cameras/scanners/mobile phones that are connected to the system
will scan the guests’ tickets. The devices will communicate with the EMTS and mark
them as present in the database.
4.5.3 Functional Requirements
(a) The system shall scan the QR/Bar code of the tickets and query the database
about the ticket status.
(b) The system shall validate that the ticket is indeed genuine and has not been can-
celled or already marked as present.
(c) The system shall allow entry only on valid tickets and show an alert message to
confirm the entry and credentials of the guest.
(d) The system shall update the database and mark the ticket status as “present”,
preventing reusage of any tickets for entry.
(e) The system shall display an alert if the ticket is not genuine/already marked pre-
sent/cancelled so that the on-ground staff may take appropriate action.
(f) The system shall also allow the staff to simply validate tickets/check status of any
ticket regardless of status. All details regarding the ticket will be displayed to the
staff. The ticket status will not be changed in this case.

4.6 Reports
4.6.1 Description
Reports containing data about ticket sales, revenue per event and per ticket category,
seat occupancy rates and no show statistics, sales agent performance (for events with
offline sales agents) should be generated by the system. The system should also allow
the organizer to track budget vs expenses of each event covering costs such as artist fees,
venue rent, equipment and logistics.
This is an added convenience for the organizers, not critical for the functionality of
the EMTS.
4.6.2 Action and Result
Within the same screen where the organizer can see all their events, the organizer will
also be able to see statistics of each event. A button labeled statistics will cause a
separate window to pop up where the statistics, reports etc. are displayed. The system
shall query the tickets database and events database and calculate the abovementioned
statistics and display them.
4.6.3 Functional Requirements
(a) The system shall display a see statistics button in the same screen that is used for
non-ordinary ticket creation.
(b) The system shall open a separate window where the reports and other statistics of
the event are displayed for the organizer.
(c) The system shall automatically calculate statistics regarding ticket sales, total
revenue of the event, revenue per ticket category and sales agent performance and
display them for the organizer.
(d) The system shall also show a live track of attendance at each event and keep a
record of no-show statistics/occupancy rates for previous events.
(e) The system shall also enable the organizer to input their expenditure of the event,
split across buckets that they may specify.
(f) The system shall enable the organizer to compare the expenditure of each event
with the revenue generated.
(g) The system shall optionally also show a cumulative budget and expense tracker
for all past events.

5 Other Non-functional Requirements
5.1 Performance Requirements
(a) The system shall be able to handle multiple users simultaneously without degra-
dation of service.
(b) The system shall complete 100% of transactions in under 5 seconds.
(c) The system shall process all information and updates with minimal latency to
ensure real-time data accuracy across all user interfaces.
5.2 Safety Requirements
5.2.1 System Reliability
(a) The system shall remain operational during peak booking periods and high-traffic
events.
(b) The system shall provide graceful error handling in case of server or network failure
to prevent data loss.
(c) Ticket confirmation shall occur only after successful payment verification.
5.2.2 Data Backup and Recovery
(a) Automatic daily backups of all databases shall be maintained.
(b) The system shall support full recovery within a defined window of 2 hours following
a critical failure.
(c) Backup copies shall be stored securely in multiple geographical locations.
5.2.3 Overbooking and Capacity Control
(a) The system shall prevent ticket sales beyond the venue’s defined capacity.
(b) Real-time seat availability shall be updated immediately after each successful book-
ing or cancellation.
(c) Entry validation systems shall block further scans once the maximum capacity of
the venue is reached.

--- PAGE 21 ---
5.2.4 Transaction Safety
(a) Duplicate payment detection and prevention mechanisms shall be implemented at
the application level.
(b) Refund processing shall be verified through a multi-step check before execution.
(c) Detailed transaction logs shall be maintained for audit and reconciliation purposes.
5.2.5 Operational Safety at Venue
(a) Each ticket shall be validated only once at the point of entry.
(b) The system shall detect and prevent the use of duplicate or previously scanned
tickets.
(c) Real-time attendance data at the venue shall be synchronized with the central
server instantaneously.
5.2.6 Logging and Monitoring
(a) All critical system actions, including bookings, cancellations, refunds, and admin-
istrative changes, shall be logged.
(b) Monitoring systems shall be maintained to detect and alert on abnormal or suspi-
cious system behavior.
5.3 Security Requirements
5.3.1 Authentication
(a) Users shall log in using secure credentials via an encrypted authentication service.
(b) Admins and organizers shall be required to use Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
for all sessions.
(c) Passwords shall follow strong complexity rules, including requirements for length
and character variety.
5.3.2 Authorization and Access Control
(a) Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) shall be implemented for Admin, Organizer,
Staff, and Customer roles.
(b) Users shall only access features and data relevant to their specific authorized role.
(c) All unauthorized access attempts shall be denied and logged for security review.

5.3.3 Data Confidentiality
(a) Personal user data shall be encrypted during transmission using HTTPS/SSL pro-
tocols.
(b) Sensitive data, including personally identifiable information (PII), shall be en-
crypted at rest in the database.
(c) Passwords shall be stored using modern, salted hashing algorithms.
5.3.4 Payment Security
(a) The system shall integrate exclusively with certified, PCI-DSS compliant secure
payment gateways.
(b) Credit and debit card details shall not be stored within the system’s local database.
(c) All payment communications shall utilize industry-standard secure encryption pro-
tocols.
5.3.5 Ticket and Fraud Protection
(a) Each ticket shall be generated with a unique, cryptographically secure QR/Bar-
code.
(b) QR codes shall be invalidated immediately after the first successful entry scan.
(c) The system shall employ algorithms to detect forged ticket patterns or duplicate
ticket IDs.
5.4 Software Quality Attributes
5.4.1 Reliability
(a) The system shall operate without crashes or incorrect logical outputs during normal
and peak usage.
(b) Core actions—booking, cancellation, and payment—must be handled atomically
to ensure data integrity.
(c) Seat availability and ticket counts shall remain synchronized to prevent overbook-
ing conflicts.
(d) The system shall feature self-recovery capabilities for minor transient errors with-
out impacting user data.
(e) Error messages displayed to users shall be clear, non-technical, and actionable.
